About Me

I welcome opportunities to collaborate and grow — please feel free to connect with me about potential roles or projects.

Data Analyst, Researcher, Psychologist, & Graphic Designer

With a strong academic foundation in experimental psychology, I bring a human-centered perspective to data — one rooted in understanding the nuances of human behavior. My professional focus lies in leveraging data analytics to uncover meaningful, actionable insights that drive better decision-making and inform strategic directions.

As a dedicated lecturer and passionate advocate for data literacy, I enjoy making data approachable. I’m skilled at breaking down complex concepts into clear, relatable ideas that help teams and stakeholders feel confident using data to make informed choices. I believe data is most powerful when it’s clearly communicated and widely understood.

My technical toolkit includes hands-on experience with R, Python, SQL, Tableau, and Power BI, which I’ve used across a range of academic and industry projects. Whether building predictive models, crafting dashboards, or telling data stories, I combine analytical precision with a strong sense of narrative to make insights impactful.

In addition to data work, I have a keen interest in graphic design. I use Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop to create clean, engaging visuals that support both academic and professional communication — from infographics and slide decks to teaching materials. This creative dimension complements my analytical work, helping to communicate insights more clearly and effectively.

I am excited to connect with others who are curious about data, driven by impact, and eager to explore how insights can shape a smarter future.
